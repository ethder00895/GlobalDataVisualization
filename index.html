    World Map — Selected Metric by Country

    Avg 2000–2020

    Each country is shaded by the average value of the metric chosen in the header filter, averaged across the selected year range. Countries with no data appear as light grey. Click any country to populate the sidebar with its latest statistics and highlight it across all charts. Insight: Northern Europe, North America, and Oceania consistently show high electricity access and growing renewable shares, while sub-Saharan Africa lags behind on access but many nations already derive most energy from renewables (hydro-dominated grids).

    Colour scale: sequential, darker = higher value  ·  Grey = no data available  ·  Use header filters to change metric and year range

    Global Renewable Energy Share

    2000–2020 trend

    Global average share of renewables in total final energy consumption, year by year. An upward trajectory signals structural progress in the energy transition. The highlighted point marks the year the trend inflected most sharply.

    Source: renewable energy share (%) averaged across all reporting countries per year  ·  Missing-data years excluded from average

    Global Electricity Generation Mix

    TWh per year

    Stacked area chart showing total electricity output (terawatt-hours) split by source: fossil fuels (amber), nuclear (blue), and renewables (green). The growing green band after 2010 reflects the rapid scale-up of solar and wind capacity worldwide.

    TWh = terawatt-hours  ·  Stacked totals summed across all reporting countries per year

    GDP per Capita vs. Renewable Share

    Latest year

    Each circle represents one country. Position encodes GDP per capita (x-axis) and renewable energy share (y-axis). Circle colour identifies the region. A common pattern is a U-shaped relationship: very poor and very rich countries both have high renewable shares (hydro-dependent developing nations and tech-advanced economies), while middle-income fossil-fuel economies cluster in the lower-middle range.

    Hover circles to see country name  ·  Selected country highlighted in amber  ·  Data: latest available year per country in selected range

    Top 10 CO₂-Emitting Countries

    Avg 2000–2020

    Horizontal bar chart ranking the ten countries with the highest average CO₂ emissions (kilotonnes) over the selected period. The list reflects long-term structural emitters — typically large-population nations or economies with heavy reliance on fossil fuels. If a specific country is selected elsewhere in the dashboard, it is highlighted in amber when it appears in the top 10. If the selected country falls outside the top 10, it is appended as an additional bar below the ranking (separated by a dashed line) to allow direct comparison with the major emitters.

    CO2 values in kilotonnes (kt)  ·  Averaged across selected year range  ·  Countries with all-null data excluded
